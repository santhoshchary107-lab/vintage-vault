Purity, Wellness, & Care Guides

Essential guides to buying, cleaning, and investing in ornaments

Gold Carats: Understanding 24K, 22K, & 18K

▼

Gold is a very soft metal in its pure form. Alloys are mixed to increase strength and hold settings:

  • 24K Gold: 99.9% Pure. Very soft, not suitable for heavy wearable jewelry. Mostly used for investment coins and bullion bars.
  • 22K Gold: 91.6% Pure. Mixed with silver, zinc, or copper. Preferred standard for heavy Indian bridal gold ornaments.
  • 18K Gold: 75.0% Pure. Highly durable, often mixed with palladium or nickel. Preferred for studded jewelry and daily wear rings.

Sterling Silver & Traditional Anklets (Payals)

▼

Traditional Indian customs dictate that gold should not be worn on the feet. Thus, anklets (Payals) and toe rings are exclusively made of silver:

  • 92.5% Sterling Standard: 92.5% pure silver mixed with 7.5% copper. This ratio provides excellent strength while retaining silver's natural high luster.
  • Oxidized Finishes: Intentional dark tarnishing applied in recesses to enhance the visibility of complex, antique patterns.

Copper Care: Protecting the Earthy Red Glow

▼

Copper is highly reactive to air and moisture, forming a natural green patina over time. Follow these steps to restore its luster:

  • Lemon & Salt Method: Rub a slice of lemon sprinkled with salt over the copper ornament to strip away oxidization instantly. Wash with warm water and dry immediately.
  • Acid Bath: For intricate filigree, submerge in warm vinegar mixed with salt for 5 minutes, scrub softly with a brush, rinse, and wipe dry.
